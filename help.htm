Computer and Internet Browser Settings

The Unified Health Communication course requires the following computer and Internet browser settings:

  • Computer screen resolution:             1024x768
  • Internet browser type:                      Internet Explorer (IE) version 6 or higher
  • IE text size setting:                          Medium
  • IE pop-up setting:                            Must be set to allow pop-ups

Setting Screen Resolution

  1. Select the Microsoft Windows Start button and select "Settings," then "Control Panel" from the menus. The "Control Panel" window will appear.
  2. Double-click on "Display." The "Display Properties" window will appear. 
  3. Select the "Settings" tab.
  4. In the "Screen Resolution" area, check to see if your resolution is set to 1024x768.  If it's not, take note of your current resolution so you can return to it, then drag the setting indicator to the right or left (depending on what your current resolution is) until you reach 1024 by 768 pixels. 
  5. Select the Apply button, then select the OK button.

(Follow the same steps to return to your preferred screen resolution.)

Setting Text Size

  1. In the Internet Explorer window, select "View" from the menu bar at the top of the window.  The "View" drop-down menu will appear.
  2. Select "Text Size," then select "Medium."

Allowing Pop-Ups

  • In the Internet Explorer window, select "Tools" from the menu bar at the top of the window.  The "Tools" drop-down menu will appear.
  • Select "Internet Options." The "Internet Options" window will appear.
  • Select the "Privacy" tab.  In the "Pop-up Blocker" area, make sure the "Block pop-ups" check box is not checked.
  • Select the OK button.

Accessing the Course Modules

To access a module, simply select one of the examining room doors.

Roll your cursor over a door to view the estimated time to complete that module.  The estimated time is shown in a word bubble coming from the male receptionist behind the desk.

Module 1 must be completed first, and Module 5 must be completed last. However, Modules 2–4 can be completed in any order.

Extras Menu

Extras Menu Items

In addition to the module content, orientation information, and navigation tools, the module screens also contain the following “extras,” available through the Extras Menu:

  • Glossary—provides a list of definitions for terms, phrases, and acronyms used in the course

Knowledge Checks

Knowledge Checks, consisting of true/false or multiple-choice questions, are provided throughout the course to help you to evaluate your understanding of the lesson content (shown below in Figure 5).  Knowledge Checks are not scored and your responses are not recorded.

You must complete Knowledge Checks to move forward in the module—the Right Arrow will remain inactive until the Knowledge Check is completed.

Completing a Knowledge Check

To complete a Knowledge Check, select the correct answer(s) and then select the Done button (indicated in Figure 5).   

Feedback

Feedback will be provided to let you know whether your answer is correct or incorrect. 

  • Feedback for Multiple-Choice Knowledge Checks—you will have two chances to answer the question correctly.  If you have not answered the question correctly after the second attempt, the correct answer will be provided, and the Right Arrow will be activated, allowing you to move forward in the module.
  • Feedback for True/False Knowledge Checks—you will have one chance to answer the question correctly.  If you have not answered the question correctly after the first attempt, the correct answer will be provided, and the Right Arrow will be activated, allowing you to move forward in the module.

Videos and Video Transcripts

Videos

Videos are used throughout the course to support and/or illustrate the concepts being presented. 

You must view videos to move forward in the module—the Right Arrow will remain inactive until the video is viewed.

Video Controls

Videos are controlled with the buttons in the video player (indicated below in Figure 6).  These control buttons are:

  • Play—select the Play button to play a video.
  • Pause—when a video is in play, the Play button becomes a Pause button.  Select the Pause button to pause the video.  Pausing a video allows you to resume playing the video at the point where it was paused.
  • Stop—select the Stop button to stop the video.  Selecting the Play button after the video has been stopped will restart the video from the beginning.
  • Jump to Beginning—select the Jump to the Beginning button to return to the beginning of the video.  Then select the Play button to play the video.
  • Jump to End—select the Jump to the End button to jump to the end of the video.
  • Progress Slider—click and hold down your left mouse button on the Progress Slider; then slide it to the left or right to move forward or backward in the video.
  • Mute—select the Mute button to mute the video.
  • Volume Slider—click and hold down your left mouse button on the Volume Slider; then slide it to the left to lower the volume or to the right to raise the volume.

Video Transcripts

A video transcript is provided with each video.  Select the Video Transcript link, located below the video player, to access the transcript.

Interactive Scenarios

Interactive scenarios are presented at the end of each of Modules 2–4, and are also used for the activities in Module 5.  Interactive scenarios put you in the role of a third-party observer, in which you are presented with a patient-provider interaction, and then asked to critique or provide guidance on the provider’s actions.  

You must complete the interactive scenarios in order to move forward in the module—the Right Arrow will remain inactive until the interactive scenario is completed.

Interactive Scenario Controls

Interactive scenarios are controlled with the buttons in the scenario window (indicated below in Figure 10).  These control buttons are:

  • Play—select the Play button to progress, scene-by-scene, through the scenario
  • Restart—select the Restart button to restart the scenario from the beginning
  • Rewind—select the Rewind button to rewind to the previous scene in the scenario 

Interactive Scenario Prompts

At the bottom of each scenario window is a prompt (indicated below in Figure 10).  This prompt provides information on how to progress through the scenario.  If you find yourself unsure of what to do next, simply look to the prompt for assistance.
