Consecutive Interpreting

  • One person speaks at a time
  • The speaker and the interpreter take turns speaking (the interpreter waits until the speaker has finished the utterance before rendering it in the other language)
  • Only a few sentences are interpreted at a time
  • Interpreter can control the flow of communication better and can more easily ask for clarification and/or repetitions
  • Usually less confusing, more accurate
  • Takes more time
  • Can disrupt speaker's flow of thoughts as he or she waits for the interpretation
  • Is the most commonly used mode in health care
  • Is the focus of this module

Simultaneous Interpreting

  • Interpreter translates with a slight lag time after the speaker begins and interprets at the same time as the speaker is talking
  • Is the most common mode used at meetings and conferences; not as commonly used in health care as the consecutive mode
  • Sometimes used in emergency situations or a mental health setting
  • Takes less time
  • More closely mimics direct patient-provider communication
  • Can be confusing with two voices speaking at once, unless special equipment is available
  • Difficult for beginning interpreters to perform well

Summary/Paraphrasing Interpreting

  • One person speaks for some time and the interpreter summarizes the main points at the end
  • Does not include every specific detail of the speakers' messages
  • Interpreter decides what to interpret and what to leave out; important information may be left out
  • Speakers don't know which messages are included in or omitted from the interpretation
  • Summary interpreting is generally avoided and usually is the practice of untrained health care interpreters

Sight Translation

  • Interpreter reads a written document and orally interprets it in the targeted language(s)

Working Effectively with an Interpreter

Modes of Interpreting

“Mode” is the term used in the interpreting field to mean style or manner. There are four modes of interpreting:

  • Consecutive interpreting
  • Simultaneous interpreting
  • Sight translation
