Message Converter

The role of Message Converter is the most basic interpreter role—the default role.

In the Message Converter role, the interpreter conveys meaning in one language literally from what has been said in another language, with no additions, omissions, or editing. Interpreters should include expressions of verbal tone as well.

Message Clarifier

In the role of Message Clarifier, the interpreter explains or makes word pictures, word analogies, or gestures of terms that can be easily understood by the listener, and then checks for the listener's understanding.

Cultural Clarifier

In the role of Cultural Clarifier, the interpreter provides a necessary cultural framework for understanding the message being interpreted and bridges the communication gap between the parties who are not a part of a common culture.

Patient Advocate

Advocacy is any action an interpreter takes on behalf of a patient outside the bounds of an interpreted interview—when an interpreter is concerned with the quality of care in addition to the quality of communication.

The role of Advocate should be used only when the patient's needs are not being met because of cultural and linguistic barriers, such as the complexity of the health care system, bias, or prejudice, as well as racism.

LEP’s Effect on Health Care

In the absence of language assistance, LEP can have an adverse affect on a patient's health care. Patients with limited English proficiency:

  • May have less access to primary care
  • May be less likely to receive follow-up appointments after Emergency Department visits
  • May be less likely to understand their diagnoses, medications, and follow-up instructions
  • May be less satisfied with care received
  • May not receive equivalent levels of preventive care

LEP can also result in poor health outcomes due to language barriers that cause inaccurate or incomplete communication of information, or inaccurate or incomplete understanding of information communicated.
