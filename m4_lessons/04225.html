Evaluating Interpreter Qualifications

There are currently no Federal health care interpreter certification standards. However, there are organizations and agencies that offer testing or assessment services for health care interpreters.

  • The National Council on Interpreting in Health Care has both a Code of Ethics and a Standards of Practice for Interpreters in Healthcare, available on their Web site
  • The American Translators Association has an accreditation program that tests and accredits translators in a variety of language pairs
  • The National Association of Judiciary Interpreters and Translators (NAJIT) has a certification examination program
  • The Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf plays a leading role in establishing a national standard for interpreters and translators through its National Testing System
  • Several states offer interpreter and translator testing and certification, such as the Washington State Department of Social and Health Services and the State of California Office of Personnel, which also includes a "Medical Interpreter Certification" under the jurisdiction of the California Judicial Council
  • Some companies offer testing of the bilingual skills of interpreters, translators, and/or staff who may be used to interpret/translate. These assessments can help identify whether individuals have sufficient bilingual fluency, but often cannot assess whether they are competent to interpret
