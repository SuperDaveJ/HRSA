Providing Language Assistance Services

Graphic Materials (i.e., Pictures)

In addition to translated written materials, graphic materials providing pictures of needed services or of characteristics of illnesses may be useful for patients with limited English proficiency and with possible low literacy skills.

Graphic materials may rely solely on images to relay a message, or may combine images and translated text. Examples include:

  • The Wong-Baker FACES Pain Rating Scale with translated text
  • A health care center sign using a universal symbol (universal symbols are symbols that are universally understood, such as an airplane, as opposed to symbols that are subject to the viewer’s interpretation, such as a cross or money)
